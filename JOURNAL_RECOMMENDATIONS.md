## ✉️ Cover Letter 模板

```
[Date]

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "The Collapse of Incentives: 
Fairness-Efficiency Trade-off in Entropy-Driven Carbon Credit Allocation for 
Distributed Waste Sorting" for consideration in [Journal Name].

Our work makes three key contributions:

1. **Theoretical Discovery**: We prove the Diminishing Marginal Entropy 
   Reduction (DMER) theorem, revealing a fundamental fairness-efficiency 
   trade-off in entropy-driven distributed systems.

2. **Mechanism Design**: We propose Federated PoER (F-PoER), achieving 71% 
   fairness improvement (Gini: 0.93→0.27, p<10⁻¹⁰⁰) while maintaining 
   environmental effectiveness.

3. **Practical Impact**: Our findings have broad implications for blockchain 
   carbon markets, participatory sensing, and commons governance.

This work aligns with [Journal Name]'s scope in [specific area]. We believe 
our interdisciplinary approach combining information theory, mechanism design, 
and environmental economics will interest your readers.

This manuscript is original, has not been published, and is not under 
consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
Hua Xiang (Corresponding Author)
School of Computer Science, Yangtze University
Email: xianghua@yangtzeu.edu.cn
```

## 🎯 Highlights 示例 (Nature Communications)

```
• Pure entropy-based incentives collapse due to extreme inequality (Gini=0.93)
• DMER theorem proves first-mover advantage in sequential Bayesian updating
• F-PoER achieves 71% fairness improvement via temporal fragmentation
• Hybrid allocation (50% baseline + 50% performance) balances equity-efficiency
• Results validated with 100-seed robustness analysis (p<10⁻¹⁰⁰)
```
